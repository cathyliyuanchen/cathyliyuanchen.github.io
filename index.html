About

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Chicago, mentored by Ben Zhao and Heather Zheng at the SAND Lab (Security, Algorithms, Networking and Data). My research interests are broadly at the intersection of ML, security and privacy, and cryptography. I am interested in studying the fundamentals of ML through attacks and defense., and investigating cryptographic solutions to security and privacy issues that are amplified by large models.
Previously, I was an AI Resident at FAIR working on post-quantum cryptanalysis with ML, mentored by Kristin Lauter and François Charton. I graduated from UC Berkeley, where I did ML research at RISELab (Real-time Intelligent Secure Explainable systems Lab), mentored by Joseph Gonzalez.
